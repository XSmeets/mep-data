Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy (the “Policy”) sets out how Browns Shoes Inc. collects, uses, discloses, and stores your personal information. For ease of reference, we refer to ourselves as Browns or simply “we”.

Our Policy applies regardless of how we collect your personal information, for example, in person, by telephone, through e-mail, or via our website.

When appropriate or necessary, we will provide you with additional information relating to the processing of your personal information before collecting your personal information.

By accepting our Policy or by providing us with personal information after you have had an opportunity to review our Policy or any supplemental notice, you consent to our processing of your personal information in accordance with our Policy and the relevant supplemental notice.

1 How to contact our Privacy Officer?

Our Privacy Officer may be reached at:

By email: privacy@brownsshoes.com

By mail: 2255 Cohen Street
Saint-Laurent, Québec, H4R 2N7

Attn: Privacy Officer.

2 How do we use your personal information?

The way we will process your personal information will depend on the nature of your interaction with us. Below you will find a description of how we process your personal information, provided however that we may further process your personal information as provided in applicable privacy laws.

2.1 When you browse our website

  • Unless you allow us to do so, we will not collect any personal information when you browse our website.

    When you set your personal preferences and want us to remember them, we will rely on the attributes of the different products you view.

  • If you want us to do so, we will collect your personal preferences derived from the attributes of the products you view on our website (for example: type of product, color, brand, size).

  • We will collect your personal information directly from you.

  • To enhance your experience when you browse our website and suggest products that would be relevant to your interests and preferences.

  • We will not be able to recommend products that may be of interest to you.

  • Store employees when you visit one of our store.

    If you need assistance after completing your transaction, customer care representatives who will log into our CRM to assist you; and

    Our fraud prevention team may have access to your personal information.

  • We rely on service providers who provide cloud-based software and infrastructure to store and process your personal information on our behalf.

    If you allow us to do so, we will share your email address as well as the different products that may be of interest to you with Meta and Google to present you with ads and promotions that would be relevant to you.

    We also rely on third party service providers to help us understand your preferences and what are the products that you may like.

  • We will retain your personal information as long as you have an account with us.

2.2 When you create an account or subscribe to our mailing list

  • We will collect your personal information when you:

    • i. create an account on our web.
    • ii. sign-in your account.
    • iii. subscribe to our mailing list;

    Your personal information will be collected using forms on our website.

  • The personal information we collect will depend on the form your are completing on our website. Broadly, we collect your name, postal address, email address, phone number, date of birth and password.

  • In most situations, we will collect your personal information directly from you.

    If you decide to create an account or sign-in your account through a social media provider, we will collect your personal information from the operator of the social media platform used to log-in your account.

  • To create your account;

    To update you on events and promotional materials, and to send requested materials;

    To process your subscription to our mailing list and send you emails.

  • You have no obligations to create an account or subscribe to our mailing list, both are on a voluntary basis. We will not be able to create your account or send you emails.

  • Store employees when you visit one of our store; and

    Our fraud prevention team may have access to your personal information.

    If you need assistance after completing your transaction, customer care representatives who will log into our CRM to assist you.

  • We rely on service providers who provide cloud-based software and infrastructure to store and process your personal information on our behalf.

    We rely on a service provider to build audiences and send our mailing list.

  • Yes, it is possible that your personal information will be communicated outside of the Province of Quebec when we communicate your personal information to one of our service providers.

  • We will retain your personal information as long as you have an account with us.

2.3 When you complete a transaction on our website

  • We will collect your personal information when you:

    • iv. complete a purchase;
    • v. sign-in your account;

    Your personal information will be collected using forms on our website.

    Your payment information is sent directly to our payment processor and we only receive and retain a confirmation token of the transaction as well as the last four digits of your credit card.

  • The personal information we collect will depend on the form your are completing on our website. Broadly, we collect your name, postal address, email address, phone number, date of birth, password, and credit card information.

    If you want us to do so, we will collect your personal preferences derived from the attributes of the products you view on our website (for example: type of product, color, brand, size).

  • In most situations, we will collect your personal information directly from you.

  • Warehouse employees who will process your order when we don’t have the product(s) you wanted in our store; and

  • We rely on service providers who provide cloud-based software and infrastructure to store and process your personal information on our behalf.

  • Yes, it is possible that your personal information will be communicated outside of the Province of Quebec when we communicate your personal information to one of our service providers.

  • In most situations, we will collect your personal information directly from you.

3 Minors under 14 years of age

We do not voluntarily collect personal information from individuals under the age of 14.

If you are a minor under the age of 14, you may not provide us with personal information without the express consent of a parent or guardian.

If you are a parent or guardian and you become aware that your child has provided us with personal information, please contact our Privacy Officer.

If we discover that we have collected personal information from someone under the age of 14 without the consent of the parent or guardian, we will take appropriate steps to delete such information from our systems.

4 How long will we retain your personal information?

We will hold your personal information until the purposes for which it was collected have been fulfilled. However, we may hold your personal information for a longer period when required in order to comply with our legal and regulatory obligations to hold or preserve information or records.

For more information on how long we hold personal information, please contact our Privacy Officer in accordance with the information provided in section 1 hereto.

You understand therefore that your consent is valid for this period, unless you exercise your right to withdraw your consent, with the consequences that such a request entails, as described below.

5 Security measures and inherent risks

We take the appropriate security measures to protect your personal information. More specifically, we have implemented physical, technical and administrative protection safeguards to ensure the protection of your personal information.

We will never contact you to request information about your credit card number or bank account. This information is only sent to Browns when you complete a transaction. If you receive a suspicious e-mail with a link to update your account information, do not click on the link. Access your account directly via our website. If you receive an unsolicited e-mail asking you to confirm or provide your password or personal or banking information, ignore the request and contact us immediately.

For security reasons and to prevent fraud, credit card orders placed through our website will only be accepted if the billing name and address match the name and address on your credit card. If the information does not match, your order will be refused, even if the transaction has been pre-authorized by your bank.

Despite these measures, given the inherent risks inherent related to the usage of computer systems, we cannot ensure or guarantee the security and confidentiality of any personal information you transmit or provide to us, and you do so at your own risk.

If you have reason to believe that personal information has been compromise, please contact our Privacy Officer.

6 What rights you have toward your personal information?

You have several rights with respect to your personal information. Specifically, you have the following rights:

  • You may ask us whether we hold any personal information about you and, if so, request access to that personal information.

    If you have an account with us, you may access your personal information by logging in your account on our website.

  • You may request to rectify any inaccurate or incomplete personal information we hold about you.

    If you have an account with us, you may update and correct your personal information by logging in your account on our website.

  • You may withdraw your consent to the communication or use of the personal information we hold about you.

    However, you understand that if you do so, we might be unable to fulfill your order or process any return or exchange for a product you bought. Depending on the extent of your consent withdrawal, we may decide to delete your account.

  • Effective on September 22, 2024, you may request that the personal information you provided us about you be communicated to you or transferred to another organization in a structured, commonly used technological format.

    You may request a copy of the personal information you have provided to us by contacting our Privacy Officer.

7 How can you exercise your rights?

To exercise any of your rights, please contact our Privacy Officer.

Our Privacy Officer will need to confirm your identity before able to process your request.

8 How can you make a complaint about our handling or processing of your personal information?

Browns takes inquiries, complaints and comments about our handling and processing of your personal information very seriously. We invite you to contact our Privacy Officer if you have any complaints or comments about how we protect your personal information.

Your complaint will be reviewed by our Privacy Officer, who will determine whether our handling of your personal information complies with our privacy program and any applicable laws.

Our objective is to do our best to resolve your complaint. We aim to answer to every complaint politely, promptly and ideally within 30 days.

You may also file a complaint with the Commission d'accès à l'information du Québec using the form available here. However, we invite you to contact our Privacy Officer first.

9 External sites or services

This Policy does not apply to any third-party websites, pages or applications that may be accessed through our products and services and we are not responsible for such third-party services. If you click or follow such links, these third-party sites or services will likely have their own privacy policies that you should review before submitting your personal information.

10 How do we update this Policy?

We may modify our Policy from time to time in our sole discretion to reflect changes in our business practices or changes in applicable laws.

We will publish a modification notice on our website, along with a summary of the upcoming changes before any modifications to our Policy enter into effect. The modifications will become effective on the date specified in the relevant change notice.

11 Approval and effective date

This Policy was approved by the Privacy Officer on September 22, 2023 and is effective as of October 24, 2023.

Summary of our policy on governance of personal information

Browns is committed to protecting your personal information throughout its life cycle in accordance with its privacy program and the requirements of applicable laws.

As such, in addition to our Privacy Policy, Browns has adopted a policy on governance of personal information, the purpose of which is to set out the principles governing the protection of personal information in our operations.

The objectives of our governance policy are to:

  • i. ensure proper, safe, compliant and transparent treatment of your personal information;
  • ii. establish guidelines relating to the processing of personal information by Browns throughout its lifecycle; and
  • iii. define roles and responsibilities of the Privacy Officer and those of our employees.

  • Because respecting your privacy is everyone's business at Browns, our Privacy Officer is supported by a multidisciplinary team that includes members of each of our business units as well as external consultants.

    Each member of our staff has a role to play in ensuring that we act in accordance with our values and our privacy program. In fact, every member of our staff is called upon to :

    • i. direct any questions or comments about how we handle or should handle your personal information to the Privacy Officer;
    • ii. treat your personal information as if it was their own personal information; and
    • iii. participate in training and awareness-raising activities on the importance of the protection of personal information.

  • Browns recognizes the importance of the protection, the confidentiality and the security of the personal information it processes in the course of business. Browns also recognizes the importance of making its employees and partners aware of this.

    Browns has prepared guidelines of good and prohibited practices regarding the ways in which we may handle your personal information, for example:

    • i. we identify the purposes and objectives for which we collect your personal information. Thereafter, your personal information is used only in accordance with those purposes;
    • ii. if we identify new purposes, we will seek your express consent to allow us to process your personal information in accordance with these new purposes, unless otherwise permitted by law;
    • iii. we only collect the minimum amount of information necessary to fulfill the identified purposes;
    • iv. we strive to obtain your consent when we collect your personal information, and where sensitive personal information is involved, we seek your express consent;
    • v. we display our privacy policy clearly and prominently when we collect your personal information through technological means;
    • vi. we ensure that our technology products or services that include privacy settings offer the highest level of privacy by default, without requiring any action on your part;
    • vii. we do not use technologies that allow identification, localization or profiling without disclosing this practice and informing you on how to activate such features;
    • viii. we ensure that your personal information is complete and up-to-date when we use it;
    • ix. we carry out privacy impact assessments when required by law, and when we consider that the processing of your personal information involves risks to your privacy;
    • x. where reasonably possible, we restrict access to your personal information only to those members of our staff and our service providers who need to have access to it in the course of our operations;
    • xi. we do not disclose your personal information to third parties without your consent, except where permitted by law.

  • Browns implemented a process to respond and to process a confidentiality incident.

    In accordance with this process, Browns must take reasonable steps to reduce the risk of serious harm and review its practices to prevent future confidentiality incident of similar nature.

    Browns registers all confidentiality incidents in an incident register and will adjust its privacy program as necessary following a confidentiality incident.

These cookies allow us to count visits and traffic sources so we can measure and improve the performance of our site. If you do not allow these cookies, you will experience less targeted advertising.
