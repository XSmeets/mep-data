What's happening in Washington

Where to see wildflowers and avoid crowds

Discover lesser known destinations where you can admire stunning blossom displays and still enjoy some solitude.

More than 250 IT leaders attended the WaTech IT Industry Forum & AI Summit

Leaders from state agencies and leading tech companies gathered on March 25 to explore how AI technology can improve state services.

Spring is asparagus season

Did you know that our state grows the most asparagus in the U.S.? Discover unique recipes from top local chefs that highlight this tasty and versatile veggie.

April is Fair Housing Month

Check out the state Human Rights Commissions resources on fair housing. Know your rights and learn how fair housing laws protect you against discrimination.

11th annual Youth Heritage Project at Ebeys Landing on Whidbey Island

This year's program lets students, teachers, and mentors explore the history, culture, and nature of our countrys oldest historical reserve. Application deadline is May 17 for students and April 26 for teachers and mentors.

Discover Washingtons stunning and diverse geology

The Department of Natural Resources launched a new website that lets people connect with our states rich and varied geologic history. Explore 100 quintessential Washington places and experience our geology up close.

Looking for SecureAccess Washington (SAW)?

SecureAccess Washington is a tool to get access to all online state government services in one place. Use a single login to access services including licensing, employment security, and paid leave.
