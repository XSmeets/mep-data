Terms of Use of Website Content

Users of the Assemblée nationale website must accept the terms of use set out below. As these may be modified at any time, users are encouraged to visit this web page regularly.

General Terms of Use

All content posted by the Assemblée nationale on its website, including

  • texts,
  • publications (promotional brochures, educational resources, etc.) and official documents, including
    • bills,
    • the Journal des débats (Hansard), and
    • other parliamentary documents,
  • images (including the Assemblée’s logo),
  • videos and audio files, and 
  • data,

is the property of the State (the Assemblée nationale). Unless otherwise indicated, the rights of the Assemblée nationale apply to all documents, data, compilations and other works that it produces, publishes, disseminates or shares, whether the documents are official or administrative, or of any other kind.

The Assemblée nationale authorizes the reproduction of these works, free of charge, under the following conditions:

  • the use is reasonable, fair, non-commercial and non-profit,
  • the content is not modified or altered,
  • the use does not adversely affect the honour or reputation of the Assemblée nationale or of the persons whose likeness or voice is found on the institution’s website, and
  • the medium of dissemination credits the Assemblée nationale du Québec as its source.

This permission is valid both for documents prepared by the Assemblée nationale and for documents prepared by a third party that has transferred the copyright or granted a user license to the Assemblée nationale.

The use of content from the Assemblée nationale is subject to Canadian intellectual property laws and to parliamentary privilege. Every user of Assemblée nationale content must comply with the legislation in force and, in particular, is responsible for determining whether a person or entity other than the Assemblée nationale has intellectual property rights in the content used.

The Assemblée nationale logo may not be used without prior permission from the Assemblée nationale.

The reproduction of Assemblée nationale, committee or subcommittee debates for the purpose of broadcasting them in whole or in part does not require the permission of the Assemblée nationale, subject to sections 48 and 49 of the Act respecting the National Assembly (CQLR, chapter A-23.1).

Any other use of these debates requires the permission of the Assemblée nationale.

Terms of Use of Content for Commercial Purposes

Content published on the Assemblée nationale website may not be used for commercial or promotional purposes without prior permission from the Assemblée nationale. This also holds for images (photos, videos, diagrams, tables, etc.) and bills.

Requests for permission must be emailed to renseignements@assnat.qc.ca.

The Assemblée nationale

  • guarantees the integrity of the information at the time it is posted on the website but not after that time; and
  • is not responsible for documents, data or other content that are modified in any way before or after they are downloaded.

In the case of discrepancies, the official text takes precedence over the content of the Assemblée nationale website.

The content on the Assemblée nationale website is provided for information purposes only.

The Assemblée nationale cannot be held responsible for the use of information contained on this website. The user is responsible for complying with all applicable obligations.
